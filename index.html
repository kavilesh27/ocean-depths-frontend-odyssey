Zone 02 — Mesopelagic

The Twilight
Zone

200 — 1,000 metres below

Sunlight fades to an ethereal blue dusk. Creatures here have evolved remarkable adaptations — enormous eyes to catch the last photons, transparent bodies to vanish, and the earliest sparks of bioluminescence.

Bioluminescence Awakens

Over 76% of ocean creatures produce their own cold light through chemical reactions. In the twilight zone, it begins as a whisper — gentle flickers used for signalling, hunting, and disguise.

The World's Largest Migration

Every night at dusk, billions of creatures ascend hundreds of metres to feed at the surface, then descend before sunrise — the largest coordinated animal movement on Earth, every single day.

Crushing Pressure

At 1,000 metres, pressure reaches 100 atmospheres — the equivalent of 100 elephants balanced on a postage stamp. These creatures' biochemistry is engineered to be indestructible.

▸ DEPTH: 200–1,000m ▸ PRESSURE: 20–100 ATM ▸ TEMPERATURE: 5–10°C ▸ LIGHT: FAINT → NONE
Zone 03 — Bathypelagic

The Midnight
Zone

1,000 — 4,000 metres below

Absolute, permanent darkness. No sunlight has ever reached here. The only illumination is the cold fire of living creatures signalling across the void.

The Perfect Hunter

The anglerfish's bioluminescent lure is a colony of symbiotic bacteria — a living lantern it carries through eternal night. It can distend its jaw to consume prey twice its own body size whole.

A World of Signals

Without sunlight, bioluminescence becomes the only language. Species flash to attract mates, communicate alarm, lure prey, and camouflage themselves against the faint down-welling light from above.

Crushing Silence

At 4,000 metres, pressure reaches 400 atmospheres. Sound travels 25% faster through these compressed waters. Whales' songs, generated at the surface, can reach these depths as deep, resonant vibrations.

▸ DEPTH: 1,000–4,000m ▸ PRESSURE: 100–400 ATM ▸ TEMPERATURE: 2–4°C ▸ SUNLIGHT: 0 PHOTONS
Zone 04 — Abyssopelagic

The Abyssal Plain

4,000 — 6,000 metres below

An alien landscape of endless flat sediment plains punctuated by submarine mountain ranges. Hydrothermal vents belch superheated water at 400°C into near-freezing darkness. Life here runs on chemistry — not light.

🦐

Vent Shrimp

Swarm hydrothermal vents in clouds of thousands, grazing on chemosynthetic bacteria. Their photoreceptors detect infrared heat radiation from vents — light invisible to any other creature on Earth.

🥒

Sea Cucumber

Processing 45kg of sediment daily, they are the abyssal plain's essential recyclers. In these depths, holothurians constitute 90% of all megafaunal biomass — the undisputed custodians of the deep.

⭐

Brittle Star

Their entire skeleton acts as a photoreceptor array — the largest compound eye in the animal kingdom, built from crystalline calcite lenses. They navigate total darkness by sensing bioluminescent flashes from metres away.

🔮

Xenophyophore

The world's largest single-celled organism at 20cm. Without a nervous system, organs, or brain, it constructs an intricate mineral shell and sustains micro-ecosystems by concentrating toxic heavy metals into harmless deposits.
